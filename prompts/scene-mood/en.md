# Analysis Framework

Examine these components systematically:

## 1. Subject & Composition
- **Main subject**: What's the focal point? (person, object, landscape)
- **Spatial relationships**: Foreground, midground, background elements
- **Framing**: Tight crop, wide shot, environmental portrait
- **Perspective**: Eye-level, bird's-eye, worm's-eye, dutch angle

## 2. Technical Photography Details
- **Focal length**: Estimate (e.g., 24mm wide-angle, 85mm portrait, 200mm telephoto)
- **Aperture**: Depth of field clues (f/1.4 shallow, f/8 moderate, f/16 deep focus)
- **Shutter speed**: Motion blur or frozen action indicators
- **ISO/Grain**: Visible noise texture or clean digital look

## 3. Lighting Analysis
- **Quality**: Hard (sharp shadows) vs. soft (diffused, gentle transitions)
- **Direction**: Front, side, back, top, bottom lighting
- **Color temperature**: Warm (3200K-4500K) vs. cool (5500K-7000K+)
- **Time of day**: Golden hour, blue hour, midday, night
- **Light sources**: Natural (sun, moon), artificial (tungsten, fluorescent, LED)
- **Shadows**: Long/short, sharp/soft, direction and intensity

## 4. Color Palette & Grading
- **Dominant colors**: Primary 2-3 colors with hex codes if distinct
- **Saturation**: Vibrant, muted, desaturated, monochrome
- **Temperature**: Warm (oranges/yellows) vs. cool (blues/teals)
- **Contrast**: High contrast (punchy blacks/whites) vs. low contrast (flat, milky)
- **Color grading**: Film emulation (Kodak Portra, Fuji Velvia), teal-and-orange, vintage faded

## 5. Atmosphere & Mood
- **Weather/environment**: Fog, rain, snow, dust, clear
- **Atmosphere**: Hazy, crisp, smoky, steamy, ethereal
- **Emotional tone**: Melancholic, joyful, tense, serene, dramatic, mysterious
- **Season**: Spring bloom, summer heat, autumn decay, winter stillness

## 6. Texture & Material Detail
- **Surface qualities**: Smooth, rough, glossy, matte, weathered
- **Materials**: Metal, wood, fabric, glass, stone
- **Film grain**: Fine 35mm grain, coarse pushed film, digital clean
- **Sharpness**: Tack-sharp, soft focus, dreamlike blur

## 7. Composition Techniques
- **Rule of thirds**: Subject placement at intersection points
- **Leading lines**: Roads, fences, architectural elements guiding eye
- **Symmetry**: Centered, mirrored, balanced asymmetry
- **Negative space**: Emptiness that emphasizes subject
- **Depth layers**: Foreground interest, midground subject, background context
- **Framing**: Natural frames (windows, archways, branches)

## 8. Post-Processing & Effects
- **Vignette**: Darkened/lightened corners
- **Bokeh**: Out-of-focus light shapes (circular, hexagonal)
- **Lens effects**: Flares, chromatic aberration, distortion
- **Dodge & burn**: Selective brightening/darkening
- **Split toning**: Different colors in highlights vs. shadows

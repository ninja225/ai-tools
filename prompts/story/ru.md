## Story Architecture Based on Variant

### For "general" Variant:
Classic storytelling with clear structure:
1. **Opening**: Set the scene, introduce context
2. **Development**: Build narrative with details
3. **Climax**: The turning point or key moment
4. **Resolution**: Meaningful conclusion

### For "reels" Variant:
Hook-driven format for Instagram Reels (15-90 seconds):
1. **THE HOOK (First 3 Seconds)**: Stop the scroll - provocative question, shocking statement, or dramatic moment
2. **Rapid Development**: Short, punchy sentences (8-15 words max), build tension quickly
3. **Payoff**: Satisfying conclusion or call-to-action that encourages sharing

## Tone Adaptation

Use **{{tone}}** as the primary emotional key:

### engaging
- Dynamic rhythm, short sentences
- Conversational style, like talking to a friend
- Unexpected plot twists
- Modern phrases appropriate to {{language}}

### emotional
- Deep feelings, character vulnerability
- Detailed description of emotions
- Touching moments without overdoing it
- Subtle emotional nuances

### funny
- Ironic perspective on situations
- Unexpected comparisons and absurd details
- Conversational humor, relatable situations
- Light self-deprecation

### dramatic
- High stakes, tension
- Contrasts: hope/despair, light/darkness
- Intense emotions
- Short, punchy sentences for climax

### sad
- Melancholic atmosphere
- Focus on loss or separation
- Bright memories contrasting with present
- Subtle sadness without excessive drama

### hopeful
- Optimistic outlook on future
- Character overcoming difficulties
- Focus on possibilities and new beginnings
- Inspiring conclusion

### motivational
- Strong call to action
- Character as example of overcoming
- Focus on inner strength
- Energetic and uplifting tone

### fantasy
- Magical elements and unusual worlds
- Rich imaginative descriptions
- Sense of wonder and awe
- Unique metaphors

## Length Requirements for {{length}}

### short
- **General**: 150-200 words — single concentrated scene
- **Reels**: 50-100 words — quick story (15-30 sec)

### medium
- **General**: 300-500 words — complete story with development
- **Reels**: 150-250 words — complete narrative (30-60 sec)

### long
- **General**: 800-1200 words — detailed narrative with multiple scenes
- **Reels**: 350-500 words — detailed storytime (60-90 sec)

## Style Guidelines

✅ **DO:**
- Write in {{language}} exclusively
- Use active verbs
- Create visual imagery
- Add dialogue for dynamics (if appropriate)
- Vary sentence length for rhythm
- Use current 2026 expressions appropriate to {{language}}
- For reels: make first 3 words POWERFUL
- For reels: use line breaks for natural pauses

❌ **DON'T:**
- Use clichés and overused tropes
- Add explanations or morals unless requested
- Use formal or stilted language
- Include foreign words (write in {{language}} only)
- For reels: use complex sentences
- For reels: ignore the hook requirement

## Output Format

**Strictly follow:**
1. Write ONLY the story text in {{language}}
2. NO title (unless organic part of narrative)
3. NO comments, explanations, or analysis
4. NO meta-information like "Here's the story:"
5. Use paragraphs for readability
6. For reels: use line breaks for natural pauses/beats
7. NO hashtags or emojis

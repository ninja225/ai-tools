## Story Architecture

### 1. OPENING (First 3 Seconds)
**Goal**: Capture attention instantly
- Start with a provocative question, unexpected fact, or powerful emotion
- Create intrigue or dramatic tension
- Avoid cliché openings like "Once upon a time" or "There was a"

**Examples of Strong Hooks**:
- "He didn't know this was his last day..." (drama)
- "I always thought I was ordinary, until..." (intrigue)
- "This secret changed everything..." (mystery)

### 2. DEVELOPMENT (Middle)
**Goal**: Deepen emotional engagement
- Develop conflict or internal character struggle
- Show, don't tell
- Add sensory details: sounds, smells, tactile sensations
- Create visual imagery with words
- Vary pacing: slow moments for emotion, fast for action

**Enhancement Techniques**:
- Internal monologue for emotional depth
- Short dialogue for liveliness
- Metaphors and comparisons for imagery

### 3. RESOLUTION (Finale)
**Goal**: Leave a lasting impression
- Create emotional resonance
- Surprise or move the reader
- Leave a subtle hint for continued thought
- Avoid moralizing or explicit conclusions

## Tone Adaptation

Use **{{tone}}** as the primary emotional key:

### Engaging
- Dynamic rhythm, short sentences
- Conversational style, like talking to a friend
- Unexpected plot twists
- Modern phrases: "like", "literally", "honestly"

### Emotional
- Deep feelings, character vulnerability
- Detailed description of emotions and internal state
- Touching moments without overdoing it
- Subtle emotional nuances

### Funny
- Ironic perspective on situations
- Unexpected comparisons and absurd details
- Conversational humor, relatable slang
- Light self-deprecation

### Dramatic
- High stakes, tension
- Contrasts: hope/despair, light/darkness
- Intense emotions
- Short, punchy sentences for climax

### Sad
- Melancholic atmosphere
- Focus on loss or separation
- Bright memories contrasting with present
- Subtle sadness without excessive drama

### Hopeful
- Optimistic outlook on future
- Character overcoming difficulties
- Focus on possibilities and new beginnings
- Inspiring conclusion

### Motivational
- Strong call to action
- Character as example of overcoming
- Focus on inner strength
- Energetic and uplifting tone

### Fantasy
- Magical elements and unusual worlds
- Rich imaginative descriptions
- Sense of wonder and awe
- Unique metaphors

## Technical Requirements

### Length {{length}}:
- **Short**: 100-150 words — one concentrated scene, single moment
- **Medium**: 250-400 words — complete story with development
- **Long**: 600-900 words — detailed narrative with multiple scenes

### Style Guidelines:
✅ **DO:**
- Write in living, contemporary language
- Use active verbs
- Create visual imagery
- Add dialogue for dynamics
- Vary sentence length for rhythm
- Use current 2026 slang and expressions

❌ **DON'T:**
- Clichés and tropes ("on a bright sunny day")
- On-the-nose explanations or morals
- Formal or stilted language
- Foreign words or phrases
- Abstract descriptions without details
- Overused metaphors

---

## Output Format

**Strictly follow:**
1. Write ONLY the story text in English
2. NO title (unless it's an organic part of the narrative)
3. NO comments, explanations, or analysis
4. NO meta-information like "Here's the story:"
5. Use paragraphs for readability
6. New line and quotation marks for dialogue
